# Source Priority

Use the highest-priority source available for each fact. Prefer stable primary
sources over summaries and copied listings.

## Highest Priority

- Artist official website or studio CV.
- Gallery artist profile.
- Museum collection page.
- Museum exhibition page.
- Official biennial, fair, or institutional page.
- Auction house lot page or sale result.

## Strong Secondary Sources

- Recognized artist databases.
- Established art press.
- Exhibition catalogues.
- Published museum annual reports.
- Gallery press releases mirrored by reputable outlets.

## Use With Caution

- Aggregated event listings.
- Unsourced biographies.
- Social media posts.
- Marketplace pages without sale verification.
- Blog posts or copied press text.

## Avoid

- Unverifiable private collector claims.
- Screenshots without original URLs.
- AI-generated summaries.
- Dead links without archived evidence.

## Confidence Guidance

Assign the confidence score to the specific fact, not the website as a whole. A
museum page confirming an exhibition may be `1.0` for the exhibition but not for
auction history or collector ownership.
